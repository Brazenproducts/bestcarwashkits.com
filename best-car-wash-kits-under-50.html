Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a foam cannon to wash my car safely?

No. A foam cannon is nice, but the safer factors are good wash technique, clean microfiber, and not grinding dirt back into the paint.

Are expensive car wash kits worth it?

Sometimes. Premium kits usually include better towels and stronger chemistry, but a solid mid-range kit is enough for most daily drivers.

What should a complete car wash kit include?

At minimum, look for wash soap, microfiber towels, wheel cleaning tools, drying support, and at least one paint-protection product.

Can one kit handle paint, wheels, and interior?

Some can, but specialist products usually perform better. The best all-around kits cover the basics while leaving room for targeted add-ons later.

How often should I replace microfiber towels?

Replace them when they get stiff, contaminated, or stop releasing dirt cleanly after washing. Towels matter more than many buyers realize.

Is a bucket wash still better than drive-through car washes?

Usually yes. A careful hand wash is gentler on paint and gives you more control over contact, chemicals, and drying.
